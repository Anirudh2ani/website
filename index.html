Call/WhatsApp to CRM concept

How a long customer conversation becomes a clear requirement.

This is the practical AI flow that can be shown to the sales and design teams.

Step What happens CRM output
1. Conversation Customer discusses needs over call, WhatsApp chat, or voice note. Raw conversation is available for review.
2. Text capture WhatsApp text is pasted/imported, or call/voice note is transcribed with permission. Conversation becomes readable text.
3. AI extraction AI identifies product, size, room, material, finish, budget, urgency, and open questions. Structured requirement draft.
4. Team review Sales/design team checks and corrects the AI draft before final save. Clean customer requirement linked to the lead/order.
5. Next action System suggests follow-up: site visit, rough estimate, design meeting, or quotation. Clear next step and reminder.

Custom workflow automation

The CRM can be built around Starwood’s exact working style.

Instead of only storing customer data, the system can move work forward automatically. Each important process can have clear triggers, next actions, reminders, approvals, and owner alerts.

Lead capture Site visit Requirement summary Quotation Design approval Production Delivery Installation After-sales Owner alerts
When something important happens New enquiry, site visit request, quotation delay, design approval, production delay, dispatch, installation, or complaint.
→
The system performs the next step Assigns work, creates a task, sends a reminder, drafts a message, requests approval, or alerts the right person.
📥

Lead enquiry workflow

Every enquiry can be captured from website, existing WhatsApp, phone, showroom, referral, dealer, architect, or campaign source.

  • Classify interest: Kitchen, Wardrobe, TV Unit, Vanity, or full interior.
  • Assign to the right sales person.
  • Create first follow-up task automatically.
  • Notify owner/manager for high-budget or urgent leads.
📐

Site visit & measurement workflow

Once a lead needs measurement, the system can create a task for the site team and keep the sales/design team updated.

  • Schedule site visit and assign measurement person.
  • Send customer confirmation/reminder.
  • Upload room photos, sizes, and notes from mobile.
  • Alert manager if measurements are not uploaded on time.
🧠

AI requirement workflow

Long call notes or WhatsApp discussions can be converted into a clean CRM requirement after salesperson review.

  • Extract product, size, material, finish, budget, urgency, and next action.
  • Highlight missing details such as exact size, appliance list, or material choice.
  • Create quotation or site visit task from the requirement.
🧾

Quotation workflow

Quotation delay is a major revenue leakage point. The CRM can make quotation creation and follow-up more disciplined.

  • Create quotation task when requirement is complete.
  • Generate quotation draft using product, size, material, finish, and budget.
  • Require approval for high discounts.
  • Create follow-up reminder after quote is shared.
🎨

Design approval workflow

Design changes and approvals can be tracked clearly before production starts.

  • Create design task after order confirmation.
  • Upload 2D/3D design files and revision notes.
  • Track customer approval or revision request.
  • Lock approved design version before production.
💳

Advance & balance payment workflow

The CRM can help management track advance, balance, and dispatch readiness.

  • Generate payment request after quotation acceptance.
  • Record advance payment and confirm order.
  • Alert sales/accounts for pending balance before dispatch.
  • Improve cashflow visibility for the owner.
🏭

Production workflow

After design approval, production can move through clear stages with owner and manager visibility.

  • Create production job after approved design.
  • Track cutting, assembly, edge banding, finishing, QC, packing, and dispatch.
  • Alert production head if any stage is delayed.
  • Create rework task if QC fails.
📱

Worker mobile workflow

Factory workers, measurement team, delivery staff, and installers can update work from mobile-friendly screens/PWA.

  • See today’s assigned tasks.
  • Mark stage complete from mobile.
  • Upload work photos or issue photos.
  • Trigger next team’s task automatically.
🚚

Delivery workflow

After QC, the dispatch and delivery process can be organized instead of being managed only through calls.

  • Create dispatch task after QC pass.
  • Schedule delivery and notify team/customer.
  • Track packed, dispatched, delivered, and pending status.
  • Alert owner if delivery commitment is at risk.
🛠️

Installation workflow

Installation can be assigned, tracked, and closed with proof instead of only verbal updates.

  • Assign installer after delivery is scheduled.
  • Installer views task on mobile.
  • Upload completion photos and pending issue notes.
  • Mark project as completed after final review.
🛡️

After-sales service workflow

Complaints, warranty, and service requests can be handled professionally with ticket-style tracking.

  • Create service ticket from customer complaint.
  • Check warranty or paid service status.
  • Assign technician and schedule visit.
  • Close ticket with photos and customer feedback.
🔔

Owner alert workflow

The owner does not need to ask everyone for updates. The system can alert only when attention is needed.

  • High-value lead received.
  • Quotation delayed or follow-up missed.
  • Order stuck in production.
  • Payment pending, delivery at risk, or complaint unresolved.
Workflow outcome: Every important step — enquiry, quotation, design, payment, production, delivery, installation, and service — can become trackable, assignable, and measurable. This reduces dependency on memory, scattered WhatsApp chats, and repeated manual follow-ups.
AI-powered workflow examples

AI can make workflows smarter, not just automatic.

The AI layer can read customer context, detect risk, suggest next actions, and help management understand patterns.

AI workflow Example trigger Business benefit
Hot lead detection Customer asks for full home interior, higher budget, and urgent timeline. Owner/manager is alerted and a senior salesperson can handle the lead.
Missing detail detection Sales note says “need kitchen, white finish, urgent” but size, budget, material, and appliance list are missing. CRM prompts salesperson to complete missing fields before quotation/design.
Delay prediction Order promised in 20 days, but cutting/production has not started on time. Manager can act early before delivery commitment is missed.
Lost lead analysis Lead marked lost after quotation or follow-up. Monthly insights show whether leads are lost due to price, slow quotation, no follow-up, competitor, or budget mismatch.
Upsell suggestion Customer is buying a modular kitchen. AI can suggest tall unit, crockery unit, breakfast counter, wardrobe, TV unit, or vanity add-ons.
Customer message drafting Order is delayed, design is ready, quotation is pending, or installation is scheduled. AI drafts a polite message that the salesperson can review and send.
